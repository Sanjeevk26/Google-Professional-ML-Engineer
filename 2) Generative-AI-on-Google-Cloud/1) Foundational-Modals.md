# Foundation Models and Multimodal AI on Google Cloud

## Overview

Foundation models are the backbone of generative AI applications. They are trained on massive amounts of existing content such as text, images, audio, and video.

These models can then generate new content, answer questions, summarize information, analyze inputs, and support real-world business workflows.

Simple meaning:

> Foundation models are large AI models trained on huge datasets and used as the base for generative AI applications.

---

## How AI Generates New Content

Generative AI learns patterns from existing content during training.

Examples of training data:

- Text
- Images
- Videos
- Audio
- Code
- Documents

After training, the result is a foundation model that can generate new outputs based on user prompts.

---

## What Is a Foundation Model?

A foundation model is a large AI model trained for broad, general-purpose tasks.

Foundation models usually require:

- Large datasets
- High computing power
- Many parameters
- Advanced training infrastructure

The number of parameters shows how much capacity the model has to learn complex patterns and store information.

In recent years, model parameters have grown from millions to billions and even trillions, making models more capable.

## Multimodal AI

Multimodal AI can process and generate content across different types of data.

Examples of modalities:

- Text
- Images
- Audio
- Video

Gemini is a multimodal model because it can understand and work with multiple input types.

Simple meaning:

> Multimodal AI can understand and generate content using more than one type of data, such as text, images, audio, and video.

---

## Why Multimodal AI Matters

Earlier AI models were often limited to one type of input, such as only text or only images.

Multimodal AI can process multiple types of information together.

This allows AI to:

- Understand richer context
- Combine information from different sources
- Perform more human-like reasoning
- Support real-world business actions
- Analyze complex situations more effectively

Example:

Gemini can use a cookie photo and generate a recipe walkthrough based on the image.

## Pre-Trained Models

A pre-trained model is trained on a large general dataset.

It has broad capabilities and can perform many general tasks.

Examples of general tasks:

- Text generation
- Summarization
- Question answering
- Code generation
- Information extraction
- Document understanding

Simple meaning:

> A pre-trained model is a general-purpose model trained on large datasets before being customized for specific use cases.

---

## Fine-Tuned Models

A fine-tuned model is a pre-trained model that is further trained on a smaller, specific dataset.

Fine-tuning helps the model perform better for a specific domain or business need.

Examples of specialized domains:

- Healthcare
- Finance
- Retail
- Insurance
- Legal
- Education

Simple meaning:

> Fine-tuning customizes a general model for a specific task, industry, or business problem.

---

## Pre-Trained vs Fine-Tuned Models

| Pre-Trained Model | Fine-Tuned Model |
|---|---|
| General-purpose | Specific-purpose |
| Trained on large broad datasets | Further trained on smaller specialized datasets |
| Good for common tasks | Good for domain-specific tasks |
| Example: general Gemini model | Example: healthcare-specific model |
| Broad capability | Higher accuracy for specific needs |

---

## Horizontal AI

Horizontal AI refers to broad AI models that can be used across many industries.

Foundation models and large language models are examples of horizontal AI.

Common horizontal AI use cases:

- Content creation
- Information synthesis
- Document summarization
- Question answering
- Conversation generation
- Code generation

Simple meaning:

> Horizontal AI solves common problems across different industries.

---

## Vertical AI

Vertical AI refers to AI solutions customized for a specific industry or niche.

Examples:

- Healthcare diagnosis
- Financial modeling
- Retail demand forecasting
- Insurance risk assessment
- Legal document review

Simple meaning:

> Vertical AI solves specialized problems for a specific industry.

---

## Horizontal AI vs Vertical AI

| Horizontal AI | Vertical AI |
|---|---|
| Broad use cases | Industry-specific use cases |
| Works across many domains | Focused on one domain |
| Usually based on foundation models | Often fine-tuned or customized |
| Example: general chatbot | Example: disease diagnosis model |

## Ways to Use Foundation Models on Google Cloud

There are three main ways to use foundation models on Google Cloud.

---

## 1. Google Cloud Console UI

This is a no-code option.

Useful for:

- Exploring models
- Testing prompts
- Trying model responses
- Quick experimentation

Best for:

- Beginners
- Business users
- Prompt testing
- Early-stage exploration

---

## 2. Gen AI Model APIs

This is a low-code option.

Developers can use APIs such as Gemini APIs with tools like `curl`.

Useful for:

- Calling models from applications
- Adding AI features to existing apps
- Automating workflows
- Building lightweight integrations

Best for:

- Developers
- Technical users
- App integrations

---

## 3. SDKs and Notebooks

This is a code-based option.

Google provides SDKs for languages such as:

- Python
- Java

These can be used with notebooks such as:

- Colab
- Workbench

They are also integrated with Vertex AI.

Useful for:

- Custom AI development
- Advanced experimentation
- Model integration
- Production-ready applications

Best for:

- ML engineers
- AI developers
- Data scientists
- Application developers

---

## Model Access Options

| Method | Type | Best For |
|---|---|---|
