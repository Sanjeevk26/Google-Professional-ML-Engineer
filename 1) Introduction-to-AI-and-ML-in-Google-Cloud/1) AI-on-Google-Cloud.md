# AI Foundations on Google Cloud

## Overview

AI can be used to solve both day-to-day business problems and complex enterprise challenges. On Google Cloud, AI solutions can combine data, machine learning, generative AI, dashboards, automation, and applications to support better decision-making.

The main idea is that AI is no longer only used for efficiency. It is also being used for innovation, automation, personalization, and real-time business optimization.

---

## Coffee on Wheels Use Case

Coffee on Wheels is an international coffee truck company operating in cities such as:

- London
- New York
- San Francisco
- Tokyo

The company wants to use AI to solve practical business challenges around sales, truck placement, routes, menus, and marketing.

---

## Main Business Challenges

### 1. Location, Selection, and Route Optimization

AI can help decide where trucks should be placed and how routes should be planned.

It can use inputs such as:

- Weather conditions
- Traffic patterns
- Local events
- Customer demand
- Popular locations
- Real-time street data

Example:

If the weather is cold or rainy, AI may suggest routes near covered areas or indoor locations.  
If a football game is happening, AI may suggest avoiding congested areas or moving trucks closer to high-demand locations.

---

### 2. Sales Forecasting and Real-Time Monitoring

AI can forecast expected sales and monitor business performance in real time.

Useful metrics include:

- Revenue
- Operating margin
- Number of trucks
- Revenue per truck
- Customer loyalty
- Item-level performance
- City-wise performance

This helps the business quickly identify what is working and what needs improvement.

---

### 3. Marketing Campaign Automation

Generative AI can help create marketing campaigns automatically.

It can generate:

- Campaign text
- Images
- Email content
- Personalized customer messages
- Menu promotion ideas

This reduces manual effort and helps the business respond faster to market conditions.

## Predictive AI

Predictive AI uses existing and historical data to predict future outcomes or classify information.

It is useful when the goal is to answer questions like:

- What will happen next?
- Which customer may churn?
- Which location may perform better?
- What will sales look like tomorrow?
- Which route is likely to be faster?

Common use cases:

- Sales forecasting
- Demand prediction
- Traffic prediction
- Customer churn prediction
- Risk scoring
- Classification

Simple meaning:

> Predictive AI analyzes existing data and predicts likely outcomes.

---

## Generative AI

Generative AI creates new content based on patterns learned from data.

It is useful when the goal is to create something new, such as:

- Text
- Images
- Videos
- Summaries
- Reports
- Chatbot responses
- Marketing campaigns

Common use cases:

- Content generation
- Email generation
- Image generation
- Summarization
- Personalized marketing
- Chatbots and assistants
- Report generation

Simple meaning:

> Generative AI creates new content and can help automate actions.

---

## Predictive AI vs Generative AI

| Predictive AI | Generative AI |
|---|---|
| Predicts outcomes | Creates new content |
| Uses historical data | Uses learned patterns |
| Good for forecasting and classification | Good for text, image, video, and content generation |
| Helps answer “what may happen?” | Helps create “what should be produced?” |
| Example: sales forecast | Example: marketing email |

---

## Using Predictive AI and Generative AI Together

Predictive AI and generative AI can be used together in the same solution.

Examples:

- Predictive AI forecasts customer churn, and generative AI helps create a sales action plan.
- Predictive AI identifies customer segments, and generative AI creates personalized marketing content.
- Predictive AI forecasts sales, and generative AI creates a business report.
- Predictive AI detects underperforming menu items, and generative AI suggests new menu ideas.

The best solution depends on the business outcome and user need.

## Google Cloud AI Architecture

Google Cloud AI architecture can be understood in three layers.

---

### 1. AI Infrastructure Layer

This is the foundation layer.

It includes:

- Compute
- Storage
- Networking
- Data infrastructure
- AI-optimized resources

This layer provides the technical base needed to build and run AI workloads.

---

### 2. AI Development Layer

This is where AI models and applications are built.

Main tool:

- Vertex AI

Vertex AI supports the end-to-end machine learning lifecycle, including:

- Model development
- Model training
- Model deployment
- Model management
- Integration with BigQuery
- Use of foundation models like Gemini

This layer is mainly useful for developers, ML engineers, data scientists, and technical teams.

---

### 3. Applications and Solutions Layer

This layer includes ready-to-use AI applications and tools.

It is useful for:

- Business users
- Analysts
- Non-technical teams
- Teams that want to quickly prototype AI ideas

This layer helps users apply AI without needing to build everything from scratch.

---

## Why Google Cloud for AI

Google Cloud is useful for AI because:

- Google has long experience using AI in products like Search, Maps, and Workspace.
- It provides tools for both predictive AI and generative AI.
- Gemini supports modern generative AI and multimodal use cases.
- Vertex AI provides an end-to-end platform for ML development.
- BigQuery connects data analytics with machine learning.
- Looker helps visualize insights.
- Google emphasizes responsible AI development and deployment.
